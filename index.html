My Fitness Journey

The Beginning: January 2023

I started my fitness journey in 2023 when I weighed 78kg. It was one of the most challenging decisions I've ever made. I was ashamed of my obesity and struggled with low self-confidence. Simple activities like climbing stairs or playing with friends left me exhausted.

The first few weeks were incredibly tough. I remember feeling embarrassed at the gym, thinking everyone was judging me. But I decided to focus on myself rather than what others might think. I started with basic bodyweight exercises and short walks, gradually increasing intensity as my stamina improved.

What kept me going was the small changes I began noticing - my clothes fitting better, having more energy throughout the day, and most importantly, feeling proud of myself for showing up every day.

How I Lost My Weight?

EAT LESS CARBS AND MOVE MORE!

These steps helped me go from 78kg to 53kg:

1. Reduce Carbohydrates

I focused on eating fewer carbohydrates and increasing physical activity. This created the calorie deficit needed for weight loss.

2. Whole Foods Focus

I prioritized whole, nutrient-dense foods like vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats while minimizing processed and refined carbs.

3. Daily Cardio

I added at least 10 minutes of cardio to every workout session, gradually increasing duration as my fitness improved.

4. Balanced Exercise

Incorporating both cardio and strength training not only aided weight loss but enhanced my overall fitness and wellbeing.

5. Sugar Reduction

Cutting down on sugar was a game-changer. I eliminated sugary drinks and desserts, focusing on natural sweetness from fruits.

6. Smart Carb Choices

When eating carbs, I chose good options like fruits, dry fruits, and whole grains instead of refined carbohydrates.

7. Planned Cheat Meals

I scheduled cheat meals weekly to stay sane while dieting, making sure they didn't turn into cheat days or weeks.

8. Protein Priority

Protein played a crucial role in my fat loss, helping me maintain muscle while losing fat and keeping me full longer.

9. Consistency is Key

Once I started seeing results, maintaining a daily routine helped me stay on track and continue making progress.

The Day I Decided to Change My Life

It was a typical Tuesday evening when I had my breaking point. I remember standing in front of the mirror after a shower, avoiding looking at my reflection as I usually did. But this time, I forced myself to look. Really look. What I saw wasn't just extra weight - I saw years of excuses, self-doubt, and missed opportunities staring back at me.

That night, I couldn't sleep. The number on the scale earlier that day - 78kg - kept flashing in my mind. I thought about how I'd avoid social gatherings because I was embarrassed about how I looked. How I'd get winded climbing a single flight of stairs. How I'd tell myself "I'll start tomorrow" every single day for years.

"The hardest step in any journey isn't the first one - it's the decision to take that first step. Everything that comes after is just putting one foot in front of the other."

The Morning Everything Changed

At 5:30am the next morning, I put on an old pair of sneakers and walked out the door. I didn't have a plan. I didn't have fancy workout clothes. I just started walking. That first "workout" lasted exactly 12 minutes before I had to stop, my legs shaking, my breath ragged. But those 12 minutes changed everything.

What most people don't understand about transformation is that it's not about motivation. Motivation fades. It's about showing up when you don't want to. It's about making the choice, every single day, to be 1% better than you were yesterday.

The Lessons I Learned Along the Way

1. Progress isn't linear. There were weeks the scale didn't move, then suddenly I'd drop 2kg. The body doesn't work on our schedule.

2. Food is fuel, not comfort. Learning to separate emotional eating from nutritional needs was my biggest breakthrough.

3. Small habits create big results. My entire transformation came down to consistent daily choices - taking the stairs, drinking water instead of soda, adding 5 more minutes to my workout.

4. The gym isn't the enemy. Those intimidating people lifting heavy weights? They're too focused on themselves to judge you. Most will actually respect you for showing up.

Today, when I look in the mirror, I don't just see a body that's changed. I see proof that I'm capable of more than I ever believed. And if I can do it, I promise - so can you.

How to Build Muscles Without Supplements

Building muscle without supplements is absolutely possible—and in many cases, it's the better path...

The idea that you need protein powders or pills to gain muscle is one of the biggest myths in fitness. Supplements may offer convenience, but they’re not essential. You can achieve an incredible transformation naturally by focusing on the basics and mastering them.

Let’s start with nutrition: Your body needs protein to rebuild and grow stronger after every workout. Instead of relying on processed supplements, focus on natural sources like eggs, paneer, chicken, lentils, oats, nuts, dairy, seeds, and legumes.

Meal planning plays a crucial role in this journey. Make sure each meal includes a solid source of protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats.

Now let’s talk training: You don’t need fancy equipment or expensive programs. Focus on compound movements like squats, push-ups, pull-ups, deadlifts, and rows.

Muscle isn't just built in the gym. It's built in your routine, in your sleep, in your discipline. Rest is not a break from progress—it’s a vital part of it.

Train hard. Eat real. Stay patient. Real strength is built from within.
